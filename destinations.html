Paris

Paris, 2024

Info Description
Population 2.1 million
Area 105.4 km²
Climate Temperate oceanic climate
Official Language French
Currency Euro (EUR)
Personal Rating ★★★★

Paris, often hailed as the "City of Light" and the epitome of romance, is a destination that captivates visitors with its timeless beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Here are some impressions one might have upon visiting Paris:

Paris boasts an array of iconic landmarks that leave a lasting impression on visitors. From the majestic Eiffel Tower, a symbol of elegance and engineering marvel, to the magnificent Notre-Dame Cathedral, each structure tells a story of Paris's grandeur and architectural prowess

As the birthplace of renowned artists like Claude Monet, Vincent van Gogh, and Pablo Picasso, Paris is a haven for art enthusiasts. The city's world-class museums, including the Louvre and Musée d'Orsay, house priceless masterpieces that showcase the evolution of art through the ages.

An invaluable tip for museum visits is that individuals under the age of 26 often enjoy free of charge admission to nearly all museums.

Crete

Crete, 2022

Info Description
Population Over 600,000
Area 8,336 km²
Climate Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers
Official Language Greek
Currency Euro (EUR)
Personal Rating ★★★

Crete, the largest of the Greek islands, is a mesmerizing destination steeped in history, natural beauty, and cultural richness.

Crete is renowned as the mythical birthplace of Zeus, the king of the Greek gods, and is steeped in ancient history. It was once home to the Minoan civilization, one of the earliest advanced societies in Europe, as evidenced by the archaeological sites of Knossos and Phaistos.

Cretan cuisine is renowned for its freshness, simplicity, and use of locally sourced ingredients. Staple foods include olive oil, herbs, vegetables, cheese, and seafood. Traditional dishes such as dakos (rusk topped with tomatoes and feta cheese), moussaka (layered eggplant dish), and souvlaki (grilled meat skewers) delight the palate of visitors.

In order to find the best beaches that are more secluded I strongly suggest going in the south of the island.

Positano

Positano, 2023

Category Information
Location Amalfi Coast, Italy
Population Approximately 3,900
Area 8.4 km²
Climate Mediterranean climate with mild winters and warm summers
Attractions
  • Iconic pastel-colored houses cascading down the cliffs
  • Path of the Gods a stunning hiking trail offering panoramic views of the coastline
Personal Rating ★★★★★

Positano, located along the breathtaking Amalfi Coast in Italy, is a charming and iconic destination known for its stunning beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture.

Positano has long been a source of inspiration for artists, writers, and filmmakers. Renowned figures such as John Steinbeck, Tennessee Williams, and Pablo Picasso have all found inspiration in Positano's beauty and charm. The town's stunning vistas and unique atmosphere continue to attract creative minds from around the world.

Positano's culinary scene is a highlight for food lovers, with an abundance of fresh seafood, locally grown produce, and traditional Mediterranean flavors. Visitors can dine in cozy trattorias, waterfront restaurants, and charming cafes, savoring dishes such as seafood risotto, homemade pasta, and limoncello, a lemon liqueur made from local Amalfi Coast lemons.

One of the most interesting things I've got to buy while on this trip was from a local based shop callled CB Positano which has everything from make-up pouches to dresses which are handmade in Positano and can only be bought from the local shop

Lisbon

Lisbon, 2023

Category Information
Population Approximately 505,000 (city); 2.8 million (metropolitan area)
Area 100.05 km²
Climate Mediterranean climate with mild, rainy winters and warm, dry summers
Official Language Portuguese
Attractions
  • Belém Tower
  • Jeronimos Monastery
  • Alfama District
  • Bairro Alto

Lisbon is characterized by its stunning cityscape, which features a mix of historic architecture, colorful buildings, and scenic viewpoints. The city is built on seven hills, providing panoramic views of the Tagus River and the surrounding landscape from various vantage points.

As one of the oldest cities in Europe, Lisbon has a rich history that dates back thousands of years. It was once a major center of trade and exploration during the Age of Discovery, with Portuguese explorers like Vasco da Gama setting sail from its shores. Visitors can explore historic neighborhoods such as Alfama, which retains its medieval charm with narrow streets and ancient buildings.

Lisbon is a paradise for food lovers, with a culinary scene that blends traditional Portuguese flavors with global influences. Visitors can sample a variety of local dishes, including seafood specialties like grilled sardines, hearty stews like cozido à portuguesa, and sweet treats like pastéis de nata (custard tarts).

Venice

Venice, 2023

Category Information
Population Approximately 260,000
Area 414.6 km² (118 islands)
Climate Humid subtropical climate with cool winters and hot, humid summers
Official Language Italian
Attractions
  • St. Mark's Square
  • Grand Canal
  • Doge's Palace
  • St. Mark's Basilica
Personal Rating ★★★★

Venice, often referred to as the "Floating City," is one of the most unique and enchanting destinations in the world.

Situated in northeastern Italy, Venice is built on a group of 118 small islands separated by canals and connected by bridges. The city is located in the Venetian Lagoon, a shallow body of water along the Adriatic Sea.

Venice is synonymous with its intricate network of canals, which serve as the city's main thoroughfares. Gondolas, traditional flat-bottomed boats propelled by gondoliers, navigate the canals, offering visitors a romantic and iconic way to explore Venice. The gondola ride provides stunning views of historic buildings, hidden passageways, and picturesque bridges.

Venetian cuisine reflects the city's maritime heritage and diverse cultural influences. Seafood dishes like sarde in saor (marinated sardines), risotto al nero di seppia (risotto with cuttlefish ink), and fritto misto (mixed fried seafood) are popular specialties. Venetian cicchetti, small savory snacks served in bars and taverns, are also a must-try.
